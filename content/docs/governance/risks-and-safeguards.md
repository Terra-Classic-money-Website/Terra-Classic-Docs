Governance is powerful because it can change the chain.

That same power creates risk. A good governance system needs user participation, validator accountability, serious proposal standards, and public proof after decisions pass.

## Low turnout risk

Low turnout means fewer active participants shape the result.

This can make governance legally valid but socially weak. It also makes it easier for a small active group to define direction while most delegators remain passive.

Safeguards:

- vote directly on important proposals
- delegate to validators that vote consistently
- review turnout before treating a result as broad consensus
- encourage proposal summaries that normal users can understand

## Validator concentration risk

If too much voting power sits with a small number of validators, governance becomes easier to coordinate, capture, or block.

Safeguards:

- avoid blindly delegating to the largest validators
- split large delegations across credible validators
- review validator voting behavior before delegating
- support reliable mid-size validators where appropriate

## Text proposal theater

Text and signaling proposals can be useful, but they can also create the illusion of progress.

A text proposal can pass without code, funding, ownership, testing, or delivery. That does not make it worthless, but it does mean voters must be clear about what passed.

Safeguards:

- separate direction from implementation
- ask for owners, milestones, and proof artifacts
- track follow-up after the vote
- avoid treating passed text as completed work

## Community pool misuse risk

Community pool spending can fund public goods. It can also become a credibility sink if proposals lack scope, milestones, custody clarity, or acceptance criteria.

Safeguards:

- require budgets to match deliverables
- prefer milestone-based funding
- verify receiving addresses and multisigs
- require public updates
- ask what happens if delivery fails

## Rushed upgrade risk

Software upgrades can improve the chain, but rushed upgrades can create halt, version mismatch, validator coordination, and exchange maintenance risk.

Safeguards:

- look for release notes
- look for testnet or dry-run evidence
- check whether validators and exchanges have upgrade instructions
- ask whether rollback or incident plans exist
- avoid approving high-impact upgrades only because they sound urgent

## Parameter risk

Governance can change parameters that affect fees, taxes, staking, slashing, deposits, voting periods, and modules.

Small parameter changes can have large second-order effects.

Safeguards:

- ask for before-and-after values
- ask who is affected
- ask whether the change is reversible
- ask what data supports the change
- avoid parameter changes without scenario analysis

## Stablecoin and market mechanism risk

Stablecoin, oracle, swap, market module, and collateral proposals carry higher risk than ordinary text proposals.

They can affect user funds, liquidity, exchange perception, regulatory posture, and chain credibility.

Safeguards:

- require clear status labels: proposed, accepted, funded, built, audited, live
- separate governance acceptance from production launch
- require oracle and liquidity assumptions to be explicit
- ask for security review before mainnet risk
- avoid claims that imply guaranteed peg, profit, or price outcome

## Information integrity risk

Users often encounter Terra Classic information through social media, chat groups, dashboards, wallets, validators, and third-party sites.

Conflicting or unofficial information can confuse users and create phishing or misdirection risk.

Safeguards:

- verify links before connecting wallets
- prefer source-linked docs and governance pages
- treat screenshots as secondary evidence
- check proposal IDs directly
- never enter seed phrases into governance sites

## The practical standard

For important governance decisions, use this rule:

> No narratives without evidence. No funding without proof loops. No authority without a clear mandate.

That standard protects users and makes Terra Classic easier to trust.
