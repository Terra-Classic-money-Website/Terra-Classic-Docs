Terra Classic is a decentralized public blockchain.

It has no central company, foundation, issuer, business development department, account manager, legal representative, or commercial authority that can sign contracts on behalf of the network.

This is the most important fact for institutional engagement.

## The short answer

If your institution wants to build on, integrate with, or propose something for Terra Classic:

1. Use the [Terra Classic forum](https://agora.terra-classic.io/) for public discussion.
2. Speak with relevant validators, developers, contributors, and community members for feedback and support.
3. Use on-chain governance if the request needs a network decision.
4. Contract only with specific legal entities or service providers, not with "Terra Classic" as a whole.

Community members and validators can support the process. They cannot officially represent Terra Classic unless they are speaking only for themselves or their own legal entity.

## What nobody can promise

No individual participant can promise:

- that Terra Classic governance will approve a proposal
- that validators will vote in a specific way
- that the chain will change parameters
- that a new asset will be listed, supported, or collateralized
- that a wallet or exchange will integrate a feature
- that a protocol product will be implemented by a certain date
- that an institution has official Terra Classic endorsement
- that "Terra Classic" has signed or accepted a commercial contract

This is not a customer-success environment. It is a public blockchain environment.

## What community members can do

Community members, validators, developers, and ecosystem participants can still provide useful support:

| Support type | What they can do |
| --- | --- |
| Orientation | Explain the chain, product status, governance process, risks, and ecosystem resources. |
| Technical review | Review integration plans, endpoint strategy, wallet flows, module behavior, and operational assumptions. |
| Proposal feedback | Help improve a forum post or governance proposal before it goes on-chain. |
| Validator outreach | Share an initiative with validators and delegators for review. |
| Implementation work | Build software if hired directly by an institution or funded through governance. |
| Infrastructure support | Provide nodes, indexing, monitoring, analytics, or operational services under their own legal terms. |

They support the process. They do not become the chain.

## When governance is required

Governance is likely required when an institutional initiative needs:

- protocol parameter changes
- new module activation
- chain software upgrades
- community pool funding
- official signaling from staked LUNC governance
- Forex Protocol rollout decisions
- Swap Protocol activation or parameter changes
- tax, fee, or oracle-policy changes
- network-wide asset support
- public-good infrastructure funding

Governance may not be required for:

- reading public chain data
- running a full node
- building an independent wallet or payment gateway
- listing LUNC or USTC on a private platform
- building a private analytics product
- contracting directly with a validator, developer, auditor, market maker, or infrastructure provider

The difference is simple: private integrations can usually proceed independently; network decisions need governance.

## What to prepare before contact

An institutional approach should be specific. Vague "partnership" messages waste time and create weak expectations.

Prepare:

| Requirement | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Entity identity | Community participants need to know who is approaching the ecosystem. |
| Jurisdiction | Legal, stablecoin, custody, payment, and securities rules vary by region. |
| Use case | Payment gateway, custody, asset issuance, liquidity, treasury, data, research, or infrastructure. |
| Requested support | Clarify whether you need technical feedback, governance support, funding, validators, liquidity, or public discussion. |
| Product status | Say whether the institution is researching, prototyping, seeking governance, or ready to build. |
| Asset model | Name the assets involved and whether they are live, proposed, collateralized, or speculative. |
| Collateral model | Required for stable-asset or Forex Protocol related proposals. |
| Liquidity plan | Needed for swaps, payments, stable assets, and listings. |
| Risk controls | Include custody, audits, oracle assumptions, limits, monitoring, and incident handling. |
| Legal boundary | Make clear who can sign contracts and who is only providing public-chain coordination. |

For payment products, also read [Payment gateway](/institutions/payment-gateway/). For stable assets, read [Collateralized stable assets](/institutions/collateralized-stable-assets/).

## Suggested public engagement path

Use a staged process.

### 1. Research first

Read:

- [For Institutions](/institutions/overview/)
- [Risk and due diligence](/institutions/risk-and-due-diligence/)
- [Governance](/governance/overview/)
- [Swap Protocol](/swap-protocol/overview/)
- [Forex Protocol](/forex-protocol/overview/)

### 2. Post a public concept

Open a clear forum topic on the [Terra Classic forum](https://agora.terra-classic.io/).

The topic should include:

- who you are
- what you want to build or propose
- what chain support you need
- what assets or modules are involved
- what benefits and risks the community should evaluate
- what is already funded by your institution
- what would require governance funding or approval

### 3. Collect feedback

Give validators, developers, delegators, community members, and independent critics time to respond.

Do not treat silence from the forum as approval.

### 4. Move to governance if needed

If the idea requires a network decision, prepare an on-chain governance proposal with:

- clear scope
- implementation owner
- milestones
- budget if funding is requested
- deliverables
- risk controls
- audit or review plan
- success metrics
- rollback or emergency process where relevant

### 5. Contract separately

If your institution hires contributors, validators, auditors, developers, liquidity providers, or infrastructure companies, contract with those parties directly.

A private contract with one contributor does not bind Terra Classic governance.

## Contact language to use

Use this framing:

> We are seeking public feedback and potential ecosystem support for an initiative involving Terra Classic. We understand Terra Classic has no central legal entity and that validators or community members cannot represent the chain as a whole. If network-level support is required, we expect to use the public forum and governance process.

Avoid this framing:

> We want to partner with Terra Classic and need someone official to approve it.

There may be no such person.

## What this page protects

This page protects both sides.

It protects institutions from assuming that a Telegram chat, validator conversation, website, or community member creates official network approval.

It protects Terra Classic from false claims that one actor can sell, sign, license, endorse, or commercially represent a decentralized chain.

The correct operating model is public, transparent, proposal-driven, and governance-aware.
