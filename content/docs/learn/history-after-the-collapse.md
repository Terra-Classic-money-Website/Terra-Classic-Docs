Terra Classic cannot be documented responsibly without acknowledging what changed after May 2022.

This page is a concise context guide, not a full historical archive.

## Before the collapse

The original Terra system used algorithmic mint/burn mechanics between LUNA and Terra stable assets. That model depended on market confidence, oracle pricing, and arbitrage behavior.

When confidence broke in May 2022, the old design helped accelerate the crisis.

## After May 2022

Terra Classic became the continuation of the original chain.

The post-collapse chain kept operating, but the product reality changed:

- LUNC became the Terra Classic native staking, governance, and gas asset.
- USTC became a legacy market-priced asset, not a guaranteed 1 USD stablecoin.
- Old stablecoin assumptions could no longer be treated as safe.
- Governance and validators became central to any credible recovery path.
- The community began exploring burns, tax policy, staking, governance reform, and new product concepts.

## Why history matters for docs

Historical context is not included to weaken Terra Classic's story.

It is included because credible documentation must explain:

- why old mint/burn assumptions are not acceptable as current product promises
- why USTC should not be described as a guaranteed stablecoin
- why proposed products need explicit status language
- why stable-asset products need stronger source discipline than ordinary pages
- why institutions and builders need evidence, not only narrative

Terra Classic's best story is not that the collapse did not happen. The stronger story is that the chain survived, constrained old failure modes, and can rebuild only through clearer governance, better engineering, and more disciplined public communication.

## Current documentation principle

When the docs describe historical mechanics, they should say they are historical.

When the docs describe proposed products, they should say they are proposed or governance-accepted but not live.

When the docs describe live behavior, they should show a source path or point to a live verification resource.
